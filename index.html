02
Organizational Design

Scaling a client success function from three people to a global team

I've built and restructured client success organizations at two companies, each time inheriting a flat, undefined function and transforming it into a tiered department with strategic project managers, defined roles, clear compensation structures, and the cross-functional systems to support them.

At one company, I grew the team from three to twenty-plus people across three distinct roles and expanded globally — personally recruiting and training talent in Malaysia. At another, I rebuilt an eight-person team from the ground up: roles, titles, compensation, process, and cross-functional collaboration, all redesigned for scale. Throughout, I stayed close to the most strategic accounts as a player-coach.

Global team management including international recruitment and training
Three-tier department architecture designed from scratch — twice
Compensation, tooling, and training infrastructure built to support scale
03
Operational Infrastructure

Building the systems that let a company scale without chaos

At a scaling GTM consulting firm, I've been the architect of the operational foundation — process documentation, systems and tooling selection, team structure design, and the people strategy that underpins everything. This isn't reactive ops management. It's proactive infrastructure building: standing up what didn't exist so the business can grow into it.

This includes supporting the Executive Leadership Team as Head of Operations, driving workforce planning, owning the internal enablement function, and most recently standing up a new business unit to test an emerging market — working alongside the founders to build something from zero inside a scaling company.

Full operational infrastructure built where none previously existed
Workforce planning, tooling, and enablement owned end-to-end
ELT support across strategy, accountability, and cross-functional alignment
04
AI-Enabled Growth

Designing a company-wide AI adoption program from the ground up

I designed and led a company-wide AI enablement program — a structured, curriculum-driven initiative to build AI fluency across an entire organization. This wasn't a training exercise. It was a change management program: structured cohorts, role-specific onboarding, a six-week cadence, and accountability built into the design from day one.

Alongside this, I drove AI process automation that eliminated manual onboarding work — collapsing a multi-step process that previously required a dedicated role and multiple meetings into an automated workflow that completes in seconds.

Company-wide AI adoption program designed and facilitated
Multi-meeting process collapsed into a single automated workflow
Curriculum, documentation, and tooling built for a non-technical audience
AI & Technology

Not just AI-aware.
AI-operational.

My conviction is simple: AI should make people better at what they do — not make them obsolete. The most powerful applications of AI aren't the ones that automate humans out of a process. They're the ones that remove the friction so people can do the work only they can do. Better. Faster. More capable.

I've spent the last several years not just learning this space, but building in it — designing enablement programs, automating workflows, selecting and managing tool stacks, and creating the documentation and training infrastructure that helps non-technical professionals actually adopt AI in their daily work. I'm constantly learning, and I bring that curiosity into every organization I work with.

About

"The organizations that thrive are the ones where leaders know what they're aiming for — and have the trust and room to get there."

I'm an operations leader based in the San Francisco Bay Area, with a career built on turning organizational complexity into clarity — and building the systems, teams, and processes that hold up as companies scale.

I've led operations at consulting and training companies, built global teams, and done this work across multiple industries and growth stages. What's stayed constant is the approach: understand what the business is really trying to do, build what it needs to get there, and make sure the people inside it have what they need to succeed.

I'm genuinely curious about AI — not as a way to replace what people do, but as a way to make them better, faster, and more capable. I find the connection between human experience and AI capability one of the most interesting opportunities in business today.

Outside of work, I'm a mom, a wife, and a friend — and those are the roles I'm most proud of. Work has taken me around the world, and so has my wanderlust. If any of that resonates, I'd love to get to know you and explore what we might build together.
